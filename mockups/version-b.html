How We Show Up

Standards behind the work, not just the work itself.

What This Module Covers

This module is about the standards behind the work, not just the work itself. At AAP, how people show up matters — professionalism, respectful behavior, ethical conduct, accountability, and how concerns are raised.

Respect and Professionalism

AAP expects employees to contribute to a workplace that is respectful, professional, and safe. That means communicating directly, treating people with dignity, and handling conflict constructively.

The non-negotiables:

  • Harassment is not tolerated
  • Bullying and disrespectful behavior are not acceptable
  • Retaliation for good-faith reporting is not acceptable
  • Dishonest, unethical, or illegal conduct is not acceptable

Ethics and Conduct

Employees are expected to use good judgment, protect trust, and conduct themselves in a way that reflects well on the company.

Speaking Up: Most workplace concerns should be raised early. Your supervisor is the first point of contact. If you're not comfortable with that, contact HR.

Need Help?

Start with your supervisor for most day-to-day concerns. Contact HR if the issue involves workplace conduct, harassment, bullying, or retaliation.
