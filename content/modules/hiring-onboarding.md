## Purpose of This Guide

This guide outlines the steps hiring managers must follow to prepare a new hire for their first day at AAP and API. From completing the Position Request in BambooHR to coordinating IT access, facilities, equipment, and department-specific needs, this process helps to support compliance, accuracy, and a smooth onboarding experience across all AAP/API divisions.

## Onboarding Task Ownership and Completion Tracking

Each new hire will have an onboarding task list assigned as applicable based on role, location, and work arrangement. Managers and assigned staff are responsible for completing their assigned tasks and marking each task as complete when finished. The Hiring Manager is responsible for monitoring overall onboarding progress and ensuring follow-up on incomplete items. HR will provide ongoing oversight of onboarding completion and report on progress, overdue items, and identified gaps.

## Onboarding Milestone Summary

| Milestone | Focus |
|---|---|
| Pre-Start | Position Request, setup requests, department readiness, Day 1 planning |
| Day 1 | Welcome, introductions, access check, role overview, first day expectations |
| By Day 3 | HR orientation, paperwork completion, early follow-up on setup and onboarding items |
| Day 7 | First-week check-in and onboarding task review |
| Day 30 | Progress review and onboarding feedback |
| Day 60 | Continued coaching and support |
| Day 90 | Onboarding completion and transition to ongoing development |

## Confirm Offer Acceptance and HR Status Update

After the written offer is signed, HR will send the candidate the required Background Check Authorization form to complete. Once completed, HR will update the candidate's status in BambooHR to **Future Employee in Process**.

- The Hiring Manager will receive an automated BambooHR notification when the status changes.
- Background check and drug screen processing begins after the authorization form is completed and the status is updated.
- Onboarding begins only after HR confirms the status update to **Future Employee in Process**.

:::callout warning "Follow up if needed"
If you have not received the BambooHR notification within 1 business day, contact HR.
:::

## Access the Position Request in BambooHR

- Log in to BambooHR.
- Search for the future employee's name.
- Select the future employee under **Employees**, not Job Candidates.
- Open the **Position Request** tab. The tab may appear under the **More** tab.
- Review the **Onboarding Process Reference Guide** as needed.

## Complete Required Position Information

Enter the new employee setup information:

- Position Request Type
- Position/Title
- Division: AAP or API
- Office Location
- Reporting Manager
- Preferred Start Date
- Preferred Name for Display -- Used for BambooHR and internal directories and, if applicable, the employee’s email display name/signature. Example: Mike McNair instead of Michael McNair.
- New Employee Setup Notes

The Preferred Start Date is important because it establishes the onboarding timeline for all parties involved, including HR and IT. The Hiring Manager should complete the Position Request as thoroughly as possible so support areas receive the information needed to prepare for the employee’s start.

:::callout info "What happens next"
Submitting the Position Request triggers notifications and internal workflows for the appropriate support areas, including HR, IT, Marketing, Facilities, and Division Leads.
:::

## Complete All Applicable Position Request Sections

Complete all applicable sections of the Position Request, including:

- Pre-Boarding Requests
- Onboarding Requests
- Warehouse Requests, if applicable
- IT Account Setup Requirements, if applicable

## Enter the Pre-Boarding Request

:::callout warning
**Important:** Request only what is needed for Day 1 or within the first 30 days for the role. The examples below are intended to help Hiring Managers decide what should be requested based on the employee’s role, work location, and expected responsibilities. 

Some pre-boarding requests may require approval before they can be processed, including but not limited to company cell phone, company credit card, AAA coverage, and company vehicle requests. Submitting the request does not guarantee approval. HR, Finance, IT, Marketing, Facilities, or Division Leadership may follow up for additional information as needed.
:::


**Company Cell Phone --** Company-issued phone/number.
- **Request when:** the role is on-call, requires frequent travel, requires MFA or authentication apps for work, or must be reachable after hours.
- **Examples:** managers, IT, sales, delivery-related leadership. 
- **Workflow:** If Company Cell Phone is marked **Yes**, an automated workflow will email a form link to the Hiring Manager email address listed on the Position Request. The Hiring Manager must complete the form. Once submitted, the completed form will route to Clint King and HR for follow-up and processing.

**Company Credit Card --** Company purchasing and travel expenses on an assigned card.
- **Request when:** The employee will book travel, pay vendors, purchase supplies, or regularly incur reimbursable business expenses.
- **Examples:** Managers, directors, and employees with regular purchases or travel responsibilities.
- **Workflow:** If Company Credit Card is marked **Yes**, the Policy Regarding Use of Company Credit Cards and Credit Card Authorization forms will be sent to the employee for signature. Once completed, the forms will route to the appropriate person to order the credit card. 

**Company Vehicle or Transfer --** New vehicle request or transfer existing vehicle assignment.
- **Request when:** The role requires regular travel between sites, deliveries, vendor visits, or frequent offsite work.
- **Examples:** Delivery-related roles, multi-site leaders, or positions with regular field travel.
- **Workflow:** If **New Vehicle Request** or **Transfer Existing Vehicle** is selected, the request will route to Mark Metzger and the Finance Department for record-keeping purposes. If **New Vehicle Request** is selected, Mark Metzger will contact the Hiring Manager for additional details needed to review and coordinate the request.


**AAA Coverage --** Roadside assistance coverage for a company vehicle or required-travel role.
- **Request when:** The employee is assigned a company vehicle or has frequent business driving requirements.
- **Workflow:** If AAA Coverage is marked **Yes**, VP of HR will contact AAA to request coverage. An initial temporary digital card will be issued and emailed to the employee. The permanent printed card will be mailed to the employee after the company receives it from AAA.


**Workstation Type (Desktop or Laptop) --** Hardware setup for the employee.

**Request when:**
- **Desktop:** Primarily onsite with a fixed workspace.
- **Laptop:** Hybrid or remote work, regular travel, or frequent meetings across sites.

*IT will confirm model and standard equipment.*

**Concur Access --** Travel and expense reporting access.
- **Request when:** The employee will travel or submit business expenses.

**Business Cards --** Printed or digital business cards.
- **Request when:** The role is customer-facing or vendor-facing, or includes recruiting, community outreach, sales, or leadership responsibilities.
- **Examples:** Sales, leadership, recruiting, and other external-facing roles.

**Marketing Introduction --** Internal or external welcome announcement support.
- **Request when:** The role is a leadership role, a customer-facing position, or when the department or work location would like a formal introduction shared.
- **Examples:** Leadership roles, customer-facing positions, or high-visibility new hires.

## Enter the Onboarding Request
Use this section to identify facility and physical-access needs for the employee.

**Building key** -- Physical key for the building/exterior access where applicable.
- **Request when:** the employee needs exterior access to the building or needs early or after-hours entry.

**Office key** -- Key for a specific office, room, cabinet, or secured interior space.
- **Request when:** employee has an assigned private office or secured workspace.

**Badge / door access** -- Badge credentials and electronic access permissions.
- **Request when:** needed for any site requiring controlled entry; specify areas/doors if role-restricted.

:::callout info
**Note:**Submitting these will automatically route a request to Facilities for setup based on location.
:::

## Enter the Warehouse Onboarding Request

*For warehouse roles only, if applicable.*

- Select warehouse role type.
- Choose warehouse location.
- Request AS400 or S2K system access.

## Enter the IT Account Setup Requirements

*If applicable.*

- Active Directory and email setup.
- AS400, S2K, Adobe, Dynamics, MapiLab.
- Zoom phone, website access, VPN.
- Remote hire equipment shipment.
- Additional IT needs notes.
- Shipping information for equipment, if necessary.

## Review & Submit the Position Request

- Review the completed Position Request for accuracy.
- If Save Changes is not visible at the bottom, click into any field on the form to activate it.
- Click Save Changes to submit the request.
- BambooHR will automatically trigger the IT ticket and related internal workflows.

:::callout warning
Double-check all fields before submitting. Incorrect entries can delay IT setup and access provisioning.
:::

## Coordinate Pre-Start Readiness
Before the employee’s first day, the Hiring Manager coordinates department readiness and follows up on applicable pre-start tasks. HR provides onboarding support and oversight as needed.

- The Hiring Manager contacts the new hire and confirm the start date, time, location, parking, dress expectations, and arrival instructions.
- Coordinate HR onboarding as applicable. 
    - Employees in the API Scottsboro Warehouse Department will meet with HR on Day 1. 
    - For employees in the API General Office, other departments, and other locations, HR orientation or HR follow-up may be scheduled separately based on the role, location, and onboarding needs.
- The Hiring Manager determines and coordinates department/team support for introductions, pre-start responsibilities, and post-hire training activities, and notifies HR if HR support or involvement is needed.
- The Hiring Manager prepares the employee’s first-day and first-week schedule.
- The Hiring Manager confirms workspace, equipment, badge/key access, and delivery arrangements are ready, as applicable, and follows up with the assigned owner as needed.
- The Hiring Manager confirms the employee’s initial assignment or early learning activities are ready.
- HR provides onboarding oversight and supports follow-up on outstanding items as needed before Day 1.

## Day 1 Expectations
On the employee’s first day, the Hiring Manager leads department onboarding activities and confirms Day 1 expectations are in place. HR completes applicable HR onboarding support and follow-up.

- The Hiring Manager welcomes the new employee and provides introductions.
- The Hiring Manager reviews the Day 1 and first-week schedule.
- The Hiring Manager reviews job expectations, department overview, reporting structure, and immediate priorities.
- The Hiring Manager confirms access is working for email, BambooHR, required systems, shared drives, and department resources, and follows up on issues with the assigned owner, as applicable.
- The Hiring Manager reviews work hours, attendance expectations, safety basics, and communication norms.
- The Hiring Manager confirms planned introductions, initial training activities, and department support are in place.
- The Hiring Manager begins role training and reviews key processes and who to contact for help.
- HR completes applicable Day 1 onboarding support and follow-up, including meeting with employees in the API Scottsboro Warehouse Department on Day 1.

## By Day 3
By Day 3, the Hiring Manager and HR review onboarding progress, confirm completion of applicable onboarding items, and follow up on any outstanding issues.

- HR confirms completion of HR orientation, as applicable. Employees in the API Scottsboro Warehouse Department will meet with HR on Day 1; for other employees, HR orientation or HR follow-up may be scheduled separately based on the role, location, and onboarding needs.
- HR confirms the employee has completed required forms and onboarding items assigned to them.
- HR provides reminders to assigned staff regarding any incomplete onboarding tasks.
- The Hiring Manager confirms required systems, tools, and support resources are available and working as needed.
- The Hiring Manager checks in with the new hire to confirm onboarding items have been completed and to identify any remaining needs.
- The Hiring Manager reviews onboarding task progress in BambooHR and follows up on any outstanding items, as needed.

## Day 7
By Day 7, the Hiring Manager and HR review first-week onboarding progress, confirm training and onboarding tasks are moving forward as expected, and follow up on any outstanding items.

- The Hiring Manager completes a first-week check-in.
- The Hiring Manager reviews the employee’s first-week experience, questions, and any early barriers.
- The Hiring Manager verifies that required training and role-specific onboarding have begun and that the correct training has been assigned to the employee.
- HR provides reporting on incomplete onboarding tasks and incomplete training items, as applicable.
- The Hiring Manager and HR review onboarding tasks and training progress and follow up on any outstanding items with the assigned owner, as needed.
- The Hiring Manager reinforces who the employee should contact for ongoing support.

## Day 30
By Day 30, the Hiring Manager and HR review onboarding progress, confirm key onboarding items have been addressed, and identify any remaining support or follow-up needs.

- The Hiring Manager completes a 30-day check-in.
- The Hiring Manager reviews progress, accomplishments, questions, and support needs.
- The Hiring Manager reinforces role expectations and department processes.
- The Hiring Manager confirms understanding of any role-specific processes, tools, or responsibilities.
- The Hiring Manager gathers feedback on the onboarding experience.
- The Hiring Manager and HR ensure any remaining onboarding items are addressed and follow up with the assigned owner as needed.

## Day 60
By Day 60, the Hiring Manager and HR review continued progress in the role, address any remaining onboarding, training, or support needs, and follow up as needed.

- The Hiring Manager completes a 60-day check-in.
- The Hiring Manager reviews continued progress and performance in the role.
- The Hiring Manager provides coaching and support as needed.
- The Hiring Manager and HR address any remaining onboarding, training, or support gaps and follow up as needed.
- The Hiring Manager confirms the employee has the tools and resources needed to continue building independence in the role.

## Day 90
By Day 90, the Hiring Manager and HR confirm onboarding is complete, complete the employee’s 90-day performance review, and identify any remaining follow-up or development needs.

- The Hiring Manager completes the employee’s 90-day performance review.
- The Hiring Manager reviews the employee’s overall transition into the role, performance to date, and readiness to move fully into ongoing performance expectations. 
- The Hiring Manager discusses strengths, progress, and any areas needing continued development or support. 
- The Hiring Manager confirms onboarding is complete and that any remaining onboarding items have been addressed. 
- The Hiring Manager and HR identify any remaining follow-up actions and transition applicable items into ongoing performance management and development planning.
