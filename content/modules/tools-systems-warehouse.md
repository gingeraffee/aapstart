## What This Module Covers

Every warehouse runs on people — but the systems are doing plenty of heavy lifting too.

This module covers the tools you will use to clock in, do your work, track tasks, request time off, and get help when technology decides to be dramatic. The goal is confidence, not keyboard panic.

## Your Systems at a Glance

You do not need to become the building's accidental IT legend. You just need to know what each system is for, how to use it for your job, and where to go when something is not working.

That is the difference between a quick fix and a fifteen-minute spiral.

## Timekeeping: PayClock

Clock in when you are ready to work. Clock out when your shift is done. Check your timecard. Speak up fast if something is wrong.

Simple rules, big consequences. Timekeeping errors are much easier to fix early than after payroll has already sprinted past them.

:::callout tip
If you forget to clock in or out, notify your supervisor right away — do not wait until the end of the day or the end of the week.
:::

## Warehouse Management System (WMS)

The WMS is the warehouse's memory.

If it is not scanned, confirmed, or recorded correctly, the system cannot help anyone. This is where tasks get tracked, product gets moved, and work gets verified. Learn the steps, follow them every time, and do not skip scans because you "know" it is right.

The system would like receipts.

## Scanning Equipment

Treat scanners like the tools they are — not like indestructible little bricks.

Handle them carefully, return them to their designated spot, and report issues when something is acting up. A dead scanner at the wrong moment can derail momentum faster than anyone enjoys.

## BambooHR

BambooHR is your HR home base.

This is where you will handle time-off requests, check PTO balances, view pay stubs, and complete onboarding tasks. It is not just a "later" system — it is part of staying organized from the start.

## Getting Help with Technology

Needing help with tech does not make you bad at your job. It makes you a person who has used technology.

When something is off, ask the right person early. The goal is to get you back to work — not to see how long you can wrestle a login issue alone out of pure spite.
