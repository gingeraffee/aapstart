## Communication Principles (CS-02)

**Core rules:**

- Be **direct, transparent, and professional** in every interaction
- Stay confident — employees appreciate honesty, including when the answer is "no"
- Use **email** for clear documentation; use **phone/in-person** for nuance and de-escalation
- In person: active listening, clarifying/open-ended questions, maintain appropriate eye contact
- **Do not guess or promise outcomes** — promise process and follow-up timelines only
- **Complaints must be documented** on the HR Incident/Complaint Report Form with the employee's statement in their own words
- **Safety concerns require full what/when/where/why/how documentation** — if it's not documented, it's like it never happened
- Follow up by every committed date; if timelines change, notify the employee promptly

**Email format standard:** Open with **"Hello [Employee Name]!"** and close with **"Thanks!"** and your signature.

## Timecard Exception Scripts (CS-01)

**Before contacting a supervisor:** Open the timecard, identify the exception, check the scheduled shift and any existing notes, and take a screenshot (WIN + Shift + S) if punches are complex or unclear.

**Missing arrival punch:**

> Subject: Timecard – Missing In Punch for [Employee Name] on [Date]
>
> Good afternoon! I noticed that [Employee Name] is missing their arrival time punch on [Date]. Can you verify what time they arrived for work that day? Thank you, [Your Name]

**Missing departure punch:**

> Subject: Timecard – Missing Out Punch for [Employee Name] on [Date]
>
> Hello! [Employee Name] forgot to clock out on [Date]. Do you know what time they went home? Thank you, [Your Name]

**Extra/off-sequence punches (attach screenshot):**

> Subject: Timecard Review Needed – [Employee Name] ([Date/Date Range])
>
> Hi! I noticed [Employee Name]'s timecard is looking a little off for [Date/Date Range]. Can you review this and let me know what the correct punches should be? I've attached a screenshot for reference. Thank you, [Your Name]

**Late arrival — no point or PTO note recorded:**

> Subject: Attendance Review – Late Arrival for [Employee Name] on [Date]
>
> Hello! I am reviewing timecards and I see that [Employee Name] was late on [Date], but there weren't any notes about a point. Was [Employee First Name] supposed to be pointed for late arrival, or did they plan to use PTO to cover the time? Thank you, [Your Name]

**Early overtime / early arrivals:**

> Subject: Overtime Check – [Employee Name] ([Date/Date Range])
>
> Good morning! I noticed [Employee Name] has accumulated overtime during [Date/Date Range], including early arrivals before the scheduled shift. Was this overtime approved/needed, or should we adjust scheduling expectations going forward? Thank you, [Your Name]

**Payroll cutoff follow-up:**

> Subject: Reminder – Timecard Verification Needed by [Deadline] ([Employee Name])
>
> Hi! Quick follow-up on [Employee Name]'s timecard for [Date/Date Range]. Payroll is processing soon — I need confirmation by [Deadline]. Can you reply with the verified in/out times (or confirm the current punches are accurate)? Thank you, [Your Name]

**After correction — close the loop:**

> Subject: Timecard Updated – [Employee Name] ([Date])
>
> Thanks for confirming. I updated [Employee Name]'s timecard for [Date] to reflect: In at [Time] and Out at [Time]. Let me know if anything else should be adjusted. Thank you, [Your Name]

:::callout warning
Escalate to HR Manager if: dispute, suspected falsification, repeated patterns, wage/hour compliance concern (off-the-clock work), missed meal break with legal implications, changes to a closed pay period, or supervisor does not respond before payroll cutoff.
:::

## Employee Concern & Complaint Scripts (CS-02)

**General concern (email):**

> Subject: Re: Your Concern
>
> Hello [Employee Name]! Thank you for reaching out. I understand your concern about [briefly restate issue]. I want to be transparent with you: [state what you know / what you can and cannot do]. Here's what I can do next: [next step]. If this requires HR Manager review, I will route it promptly. I will follow up with you by [Date]. Thanks!

**General concern (in person/phone) — active listening:**

1. *"Thanks for bringing this to me. I want to make sure I understand."*
2. *"Can you walk me through what happened from start to finish?"*
3. *"So what I'm hearing is [restate in one sentence]. Did I get that right?"*
4. *"Here's what I can tell you right now..."*
5. *"Here's what will happen next, and when you can expect an update."*

**Complaint intake (in person/phone):**

*"Thank you for bringing this forward. I'm going to document what you're reporting so it can be reviewed appropriately. I'll need: what happened, when and where, who was involved, and whether there were witnesses. I'll also need your statement in your own words for the complaint form. Once the paperwork is completed, I'll turn it over to the HR Manager with a synopsis so an investigation can be opened."*

**Pay question (always route — never resolve yourself):**

> Subject: Re: Pay Question
>
> Hello [Employee Name]! Pay questions need to be reviewed directly by the HR Manager to ensure accuracy. I'm forwarding your question now. To help with review, please include the pay period date and whether this relates to hours, overtime, bonus, or a deduction. Thanks!

**Harassment/discrimination/retaliation (immediate escalation):**

*"Thank you for telling me. I'm going to document what you reported and route this to the HR Manager immediately."* → Do NOT investigate. Intake + documentation + handoff only. Advise employee to preserve any evidence (texts, emails, screenshots).

**Employee wants a point excused:**

*"API/AAP uses a no-fault point system for cases just like this. Did you want to see if you have any PTO you can use instead of taking the point? Remember: vacation must be taken in 2-hour increments and personal time in 1-hour increments."*

## Escalation Routing (ES-01 / CS-04)

**Escalation routing table:**

| Issue Type | Owner | Urgency |
|------------|-------|---------|
| Pay discrepancy | HR Manager | Immediate |
| Safety hazard / injury | HR Manager + Safety Lead | Immediate |
| Harassment / discrimination / retaliation | HR Manager | Immediate |
| Threats / violence | HR Manager + Site Leadership | Immediate |
| General complaint / workplace conflict | HR Manager | Same Day |
| FMLA / medical / leave | HR Manager | Immediate |
| Attendance point exception | HR Manager (if exception) | Same Day |
| PTO balance discrepancy | HR Manager | Next Business Day |
| Performance dispute | HR Manager | Next Business Day |
| Policy exception request | HR Manager | Next Business Day |

**Every internal escalation message must include:**

- Employee name + ID + location/department
- Category + urgency level
- 1–2 sentence synopsis
- Key facts (dates, times, locations, who was involved)
- Attachments (complaint form, statements, screenshots)
- Action taken so far + requested owner action + deadline
