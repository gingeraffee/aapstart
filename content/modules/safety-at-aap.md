## What This Module Covers

Safety applies to everyone.

This module covers the universal safety expectations that belong in shared onboarding: reporting hazards, injuries, and incidents promptly, following training and procedures, and using good judgment in the workplace.

## General Safety Expectations

AAP has a workplace safety program and provides safety training. Employees are expected to obey safety rules, use caution in all work activities, and report unsafe conditions promptly.

For onboarding, the core message is simple:

- Report hazards, injuries, and incidents promptly
- Follow posted procedures and training
- Pay attention to slips, trips, and fall hazards
- Practice safe work habits
- Ask questions when unsure
- Understand that safety applies to every employee

## Reporting Injuries and Incidents

Work-related injuries should be reported **immediately** to your supervisor, no matter how minor they may seem.

:::callout warning
Do not wait. Even small injuries should be reported right away. Delayed reporting can create complications for you and for the company.
:::

## Security and Workplace Awareness

AAP also has policies related to visitors, computer and email use, workplace monitoring, and workplace violence prevention. The key message is to use good judgment, follow workplace rules, and speak up when something feels unsafe or out of bounds.

## Need Help?

Start with your **supervisor** for immediate safety concerns. Use **HR** when the issue requires follow-up, support, or escalation.


:::track [warehouse]
## Warehouse Safety & OSHA Highlights

Warehouse teams have additional safety requirements on top of the shared AAP expectations.

- Closed-toe, closed-heel footwear is required on the floor.
- Report all injuries and near-misses immediately to your supervisor.
- Do not operate forklifts or pallet jacks unless you are currently certified.
- Use safe lifting form: assess the load, bend at the knees, keep the load close, and avoid twisting.
- Review Safety Data Sheets (SDS) before handling unfamiliar materials.

If any task feels unsafe, stop and escalate before continuing.
:::
