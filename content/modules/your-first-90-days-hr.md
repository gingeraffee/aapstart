## What This Module Covers

Your first 90 days set the tone for your time at AAP. This module breaks those three months into clear phases — what to focus on, what success looks like, and how to ask for help when you need it.

---

## Days 1–30: Learn, Listen, and Get Set Up

Your first month is about orientation, not performance pressure. The priority is to build a solid foundation.

:::callout tip
You're not expected to have everything figured out in month one. Ask questions early — it's far better to ask than to guess on something HR-related.
:::

**What to focus on:**

- Complete all AAP Start modules and setup tasks
- Learn the core systems: BambooHR, Paylocity, and LinkedIn Learning
- Shadow your supervisor and observe how HR processes flow day-to-day
- Get to know your colleagues and learn who does what
- Start to understand the HR calendar: payroll deadlines, benefits open enrollment windows, and recurring reports
- Review the Employee Handbook and key HR policies so you can reference them confidently

**A good question to ask yourself at 30 days:** *Do I know where to find things, who to ask, and how the core systems work?*

---

## Days 31–60: Build Confidence With Core Tasks

By month two, you should be moving from observation to participation. You'll start handling routine HR tasks with increasing independence.

**What to focus on:**

- Assist with onboarding coordination for new hires — scheduling, paperwork tracking, system access
- Process basic HR administrative tasks (document filing, data entry, meeting scheduling) accurately and on time
- Learn how to handle common employee questions — and when to escalate to your supervisor
- Begin to recognize patterns: what questions come up often? What processes repeat each pay period?
- Identify any gaps in your knowledge and bring them to your supervisor proactively

:::callout note
"Increasing independence" doesn't mean doing things alone without support. It means completing tasks without needing step-by-step guidance on things you've done before.
:::

---

## Days 61–90: Contribute and Connect

Month three is about deepening your competence and becoming a reliable member of the HR team.

**What to focus on:**

- Handle routine HR administrative tasks confidently and independently
- Build relationships across the organization — other departments will rely on HR for support, and knowing people makes that easier
- Contribute to any HR projects or initiatives underway
- Bring forward process improvements or questions you've noticed — your fresh perspective is valuable
- Prepare for your 90-day check-in with your supervisor: come ready to discuss what's going well and where you want to grow

**A good question to ask yourself at 90 days:** *Am I a reliable, trusted resource for the people I support?*

---

## Your 90-Day Check-In

At the end of your first 90 days, you'll have a check-in conversation with your supervisor. This is a two-way conversation — your supervisor will share feedback, and you'll have the chance to share yours.

Come prepared to discuss:
- What's going well
- Where you want more support or development
- Any questions about your role or expectations going forward

:::callout tip
Write down observations and questions throughout your first 90 days. It's easy to forget things, and having notes makes check-ins much more productive.
:::

---

## When to Ask for Help

**During your first 90 days, your supervisor is always your first resource.** There is no question too small, especially early on.

HR work often involves confidential or sensitive information. When in doubt about how to handle something, stop and ask — acting on an incorrect assumption in HR can have real consequences for employees.

For HR policy questions: **Nicole Thornton, HR Manager**
For system access issues: **Start with your supervisor**
