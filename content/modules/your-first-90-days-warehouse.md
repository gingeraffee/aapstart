## Before You Hit Full Speed

You are brand new. That means everything is unfamiliar, most names have not stuck yet, and the job still feels like a lot of moving parts. That is exactly where you are supposed to be right now. This module is the cheat sheet — the stuff that helps your first month feel less like guesswork and more like progress.

## Your First Week

Your first week has a rhythm to it — here is how it breaks down:

**Day 1 — Orientation.** This is the big-picture day. You will get the lay of the land, meet key people, and start learning how things work around here. Nothing hands-on yet — just absorb what you can.

**Days 2–4 — Working with a trainer.** You will be paired with someone who knows the floor. Watch how they work, ask questions as they come up, and start doing things alongside them. You are not expected to memorize everything — you are expected to stay engaged and keep learning.

**Day 5 — Your first day working alone.** By Friday, you will start handling things on your own. That does not mean you should stop asking questions — it just means you have enough of a foundation to start building on it.

## How to Ask for Help

Questions are part of onboarding. Silence is where expensive mistakes like to grow. Ask when you are unsure. Ask before you guess. Ask before a small issue becomes a larger one with witnesses. Nobody is judging you for not knowing something on day three.

Here is a quick guide to who handles what:

- **Need help with benefits, leave, policies, or HR-related questions?** Call, email, or find Nicole Thornton. She is always happy to help.
- **Need to take the day off?** Enter your request in BambooHR to notify your supervisor and HR at the same time. It is just a few taps and you are done — you even get an email confirmation when your request is approved or denied.
- **Questions about processes on the job or detailed step-by-step guidance?** Find your trainer and they will be more than happy to give you a refresher.
- **Questions about performance, day-to-day direction, or warehouse-related concerns?** Your supervisor is your go-to contact for these questions.
- **Having equipment issues or trouble accessing your systems?** Reach out to your IT contact for support and troubleshooting.

## Your 30-Day Check-In

Around the 30-day mark, your supervisor will sit down with you for a check-in. This is not a test and it is not a trapdoor — it is a structured conversation designed to help both of you understand where you are and what comes next.

Your supervisor uses a framework for this conversation, so it is consistent and thorough. Here is what to expect:

**A hands-on knowledge check.** Your supervisor will walk through some of the core tasks you have been trained on to see how you are doing in practice. This is not a pop quiz — it is a chance to show what you have picked up and flag anything that needs a refresher.

**A self-assessment conversation.** You will talk through how confident you feel across different parts of the job. Be honest here — saying "I am still shaky on this" is more useful than pretending everything is fine. That honesty is what gets you the right support.

**A safety and compliance review.** Are you following PPE protocols consistently? Do you know what to do in an emergency? Your supervisor will check in on the safety habits you have been building.

**A resource and systems check.** Can you navigate BambooHR? Do you know who your contacts are for HR, IT, and training? If anything slipped through the cracks during onboarding, this is where it gets caught.

**Goal-setting for days 31 through 90.** The first month was about learning the foundation. The next stretch is about building on it. You and your supervisor will set specific goals so the next 60 days have direction — not just "keep doing what you are doing."

**Your feedback on the training.** This one matters more than you think. What worked? What did not? What would have helped you learn faster? Your answers feed directly into improving onboarding for the people who come after you.

### How to Prepare

Come ready to talk. Here are some things worth reflecting on before the conversation:

- **Job confidence** — What parts of the job feel solid? Where are you still unsure?
- **Safety** — Are you clear on PPE requirements, emergency procedures, and floor protocols?
- **Systems and resources** — Can you get into everything you need? Do you know where to find policies and forms?
- **Team and culture** — Are you comfortable asking coworkers and your trainer for help?
- **Training feedback** — What would have made your first month smoother?

:::callout tip
You do not need to have perfect answers. The point is to show up thinking about where you are and where you want to go. That is what makes the check-in valuable for both sides.
:::

## Building Good Habits from Day One

The habits you build early tend to stick. Build good ones.

- Show up ready
- Scan everything
- Keep your area clean
- Speak up early when something is off
- Treat people well
- Ask before assuming

Nothing here is glamorous. But this is the stuff that makes someone dependable, respected, and a whole lot easier to work with.

:::callout tip
Consistent and reliable beats fast and inconsistent every single time. Speed comes with repetition. Accuracy and habits come with intention.
:::
