## What This Module Covers

The first month is about learning the job, building confidence, and getting your footing — without feeling like you are supposed to be a seasoned pro by Friday.

This module covers what to expect in your first week, who to go to for help on the floor, what the 30-day check-in looks like, and how to build habits that will actually serve you long-term.

## Your First Week

Your first week will probably feel like a mix of learning, observing, asking, and trying to remember twelve things at once. That is normal.

Nobody expects you to be at full speed on day one. The expectation is that you pay attention, follow direction, and stay curious enough to keep learning.

## Who's Who on the Floor

Knowing who to ask saves a lot of wandering and a surprising amount of stress.

| Who | Their lane |
|---|---|
| Your supervisor | Day-to-day direction, attendance, performance feedback, escalations |
| Your trainer | Process walkthroughs, hands-on guidance, step-by-step questions |
| HR | Benefits, leave, sensitive workplace concerns, policy questions |
| IT | Login issues, equipment problems, system access |

Learn who handles what early, and life gets easier fast.

## How to Ask for Help

Questions are part of onboarding. Silence is where expensive mistakes like to grow.

Ask when you are unsure. Ask before you guess. Ask before a small issue becomes a larger one with witnesses. Nobody is judging you for not knowing something on day three.

:::callout tip
The question you are embarrassed to ask on day one is usually the question someone else needed to ask and didn't. Ask it.
:::

## Your 30-Day Check-In

At the 30-day mark, you will have a check-in conversation with your supervisor. This is not a trapdoor situation.

It is a real conversation about what is going well, what still feels uncertain, and what support would help you keep improving. Come prepared with questions — that is exactly what the check-in is for.

Some things worth reflecting on before that conversation:

- What parts of the job feel solid?
- What are you still unsure about?
- Is there anything about the process or the work that is confusing or inconsistent?
- Is there anything you need that you do not currently have?

## Building Good Habits from Day One

The habits you build early tend to stick. Build good ones.

- Show up ready
- Scan everything
- Keep your area clean
- Speak up early when something is off
- Treat people well
- Ask before assuming

Nothing here is glamorous. But this is the stuff that makes someone dependable, respected, and a whole lot easier to work with.

:::callout tip
Consistent and reliable beats fast and inconsistent every single time. Speed comes with repetition. Accuracy and habits come with intention.
:::
