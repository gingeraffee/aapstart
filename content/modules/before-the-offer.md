## The Recruiting Workflow (HRA-02)

Recruiting isn't a project you start and finish — it's a cycle that runs in the background all the time. Your piece of it starts when a supervisor changes a candidate's status in BambooHR. That status change is your trigger. You don't wait for an email, a Teams message, or someone to tap you on the shoulder. You check BambooHR daily, and when a status moves, you move with it.

Here's how the workflow plays out from first contact to Day 1:

**1. "Schedule Interview" status appears.** This means the supervisor reviewed the application and wants to meet the candidate. Your job: call the applicant and coordinate a date, time, and location. Don't email first — call. It's faster and it sets the tone that AAP is responsive.

**2. Send the confirmation email.** In BambooHR, search for the applicant (upper-right search bar) → open their screening page → go to Emails → select the correct interview confirmation template → send. Make sure the date, time, and location are all included. A confirmation email without the details isn't a confirmation — it's a source of confusion.

**3. "Candidate Selected For Hire" status appears.** The supervisor has made their decision. Now you call the candidate with a verbal offer. This is a conversation, not a voicemail. If you can't reach them, try again — but don't leave offer details in a message.

**4. Verbal offer accepted → send the offer letter.** Open the candidate's profile → status drop-down → **Create Offer Letter** → select the correct template → fill in every detail → send for signature. Double-check the pay rate, job title, and start date before you hit send. A wrong number here is a bad way to start a relationship.

**5. Offer letter signed → initiate screening immediately.** Background check and drug screening kick off the moment the signature is in. Not tomorrow. Not after lunch. Now. The clock starts ticking on the 3-business-day drug screening window as soon as that letter is signed.

**6. Complete reference checks (RM-01).** These can run in parallel with the screening. Don't wait for results to start making calls — use the time wisely.

**7. Screening results received and clear → schedule the start date.** Tuesdays are preferred for new hire start dates. Never confirm a start date until you have clear screening results in hand. Telling someone to show up Monday when you're still waiting on a drug test result is a problem you don't want.

**8. Send the New Hire Packet.** Use the correct BambooHR template and include any state-specific tax forms if the employee isn't in Tennessee or Alabama.

**9. Confirm everything is filed.** All pre-hire screenings, reference check documentation, and offer letter should be stored in BambooHR before Day 1. If it's not in the system, it's not done.

:::callout warning
Escalate immediately if: a candidate declines after verbally accepting, offer terms need an exception, a background or drug screen comes back with a concern, a reference raises a red flag, or the candidate requests an accommodation.
:::

## Background & Drug Screening (HRA-03)

This is one of those processes where timing matters more than almost anything else. The screening gets triggered the moment an offer letter is signed — and the drug test has to be completed within **3 business days**. Not 3 days whenever you get around to it. Three business days from signature.

**The rules are the same for every candidate:**

- Drug tests are **free** — the candidate doesn't pay anything
- They must bring a **photo ID** to the testing site
- Update BambooHR status to **"Pending Drug Screening Results"** as soon as you initiate the screening
- **Never confirm a start date until results are back.** This is non-negotiable. A start date without clear results is a liability.

### Memphis Candidates

Memphis has access to LabCorp facilities, so the screening runs electronically through Employvio. You place the order, the candidate gets an email invitation, and they walk into a LabCorp location on their own timeline (within the 3-day window).

**Steps:** In Employvio → **Order → Order w/ Invitation → Standard → A La Carte** → check **(AF) LabCorp – 10 Panel** → Next → select the **Background Check Authorization** template → enter the candidate's info → Send Invitation.

The candidate receives an email with instructions and a list of LabCorp locations near them. Results come back through Employvio.

### Scottsboro Candidates

Scottsboro doesn't have an approved LabCorp facility within 25 miles, so candidates go to a local provider instead — The Family Life Center. This means a few extra steps on your end since you're coordinating between BambooHR, the candidate, the testing site, and Employvio.

**Steps:**

1. When the offer letter is signed, send the **APIS Drug Screening Instructions** template from BambooHR — this tells the candidate where to go, what to bring, and the hours
2. Update BambooHR status to **"Pending Drug Screening Results"**
3. The candidate goes to **The Family Life Center** — 211 S. Market Street, Scottsboro, AL 35768 (Mon–Fri 8am–5pm, closed 12–1pm for lunch). They must bring a **photo ID**
4. In Employvio: **Order → Order → Standard → Next** → enter candidate info → select **Standard Screening – 1 County** → Submit

**Where results show up:** Memphis results come through Employvio as a PDF. Scottsboro results come through Phoenix LIS (log in via Proton Pass or ask Nicole for access).

:::callout warning
Escalate immediately if: the candidate can't complete the test within 3 days, the testing site is inaccessible, results are delayed, results come back potentially positive or failed, there's an identity mismatch, or anything feels off.
:::

## Reference Checks (RM-01)

Reference checks are one of those tasks that seem simple until you're on the phone and someone says something you weren't expecting. The rules are straightforward — but the conversations aren't always.

**The minimum:** You need **3 successful contacts** — at least 1 employer reference and at least 1 personal reference. Candidates typically provide around 6 references, so you're selecting 3 to call. If your first picks don't answer, keep going down the list until you hit 3.

**Before you call, have ready:** Your full name, your callback number, and the position the candidate applied for. You'll introduce yourself and explain why you're calling — keep it professional but conversational. Nobody likes being interrogated.

### Employer Reference Questions

These are your baseline questions for any former employer:

- Dates of employment
- Attendance and punctuality
- Work quality and reliability
- Teamwork and communication
- Eligible for rehire? (only if they're no longer employed there)

**For warehouse roles, add 1–3 of these:** Productivity and accuracy | Safety mindset | SOP compliance | Shift reliability | Equipment experience

**For administrative roles, add 1–3 of these:** Attention to detail | Handling confidential information | Communication skills | Managing competing priorities | Professionalism

### Personal Reference Questions

Personal references give you a different angle — how the candidate shows up outside of work.

- How long have you known them, and how?
- How would you describe their reliability and follow-through?
- How do they handle stress or feedback?
- How would you describe their communication and professionalism?
- Any concerns that might be relevant to a job?

### What You Can Never Ask — Or Document

This part is serious. Protected characteristics are completely off-limits — you don't ask about them, and if a reference brings one up unprompted, you redirect immediately and **do not write it down.**

The list: medical conditions or disability, age, religion, family status, pregnancy, national origin, or any other protected characteristic.

If a reference says *"Oh, she just had a baby!"* — your response is to steer back to job-related topics. Not to note it. Not to acknowledge it. Redirect and move on.

:::callout warning
If protected information comes up during a reference check, redirect to job-related topics immediately. Never document protected characteristics — even if the reference volunteers them.
:::

### Filing Your Reference Checks

**File name format:** `YYYY-DD-MM - Reference Check - FirstName LastName`

**What to include in each reference note:** Reference name and phone number, date you contacted them, your full name, and a concise job-related summary of what they said.

**Where it goes:** Upload to BambooHR → **Resumes, Applications and Pre-Hire Records** → mark the reference check task complete in BambooHR.

**Escalate if:** You can't reach 3 references after multiple attempts, a reference raises a serious concern (safety, theft, harassment, dishonesty, policy violations), the candidate is ineligible for rehire at a previous employer, or the information you're hearing conflicts with what's on the application.
